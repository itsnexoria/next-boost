How to Get Free Discord Members for Your Server

Fake member counts don't build a community. Here's how to fill your Discord with real people who actually joined on purpose.

📅 Updated June 2026·⏱️ 6 min read

Why a fake member count doesn't help your Discord server

It's possible to buy a server full of bot accounts to inflate your member count, but it does nothing for the thing that actually matters: an active, engaged community. A high member count with zero activity in chat is an obvious red flag to anyone who joins and looks around — it can actually make a server look dead rather than thriving.

A server that grows from real, individually-joined members at least has a chance of generating real conversation, even if most members start out passive. That's the gap engagement exchange is meant to fill.

  1. Sign up free and get 100 points to start.
  2. Join other creators' Discord servers to earn points — each join task gives you points once you've joined the server.
  3. Post your own task with your server's invite link, set how many new members you want, and how many points you're offering per join.
  4. Real Discord users join your server using their own accounts, no bots involved.

Making the most of new members once they join

Have a welcome channel ready

New members joining through a task are still real people browsing the server — a clear welcome message, rules channel, and a few active channels make it more likely they stick around instead of leaving right after.

Use roles to make joining feel rewarding

A simple auto-role or welcome message that gives new members something to do (an intro channel, a roles-select message) turns a points-driven join into an actual first interaction with your community.

Run a join task again after big updates

If you add new content, channels, or events to your server, running another join task is a low-effort way to bring in a fresh batch of members at that moment.
